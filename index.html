CONTENT STRATEGY —
SURVEY-INFORMED

Every content decision in this section is grounded directly in the survey data. The client told us what they believe in, what they fear, what they want people to feel, and what must never be claimed. This is the translation of those answers into a repeatable content system.

The Non-Negotiable Content Rules (From Survey)

The survey established clear red lines. Never claim "India's largest," "Olympic-level certified," "International standard," "Best in India," or "Fully operational" without documented proof. The preferred alternative to "Olympic-standard" is "World Climbing" — as the sport is governed by World Climbing, not the IOC directly. "Top Notch, Leading Training Center" was offered as safe superlative language. The dormitory stay must not be mentioned publicly. Free pricing must never be shown publicly. Achievements and awards must be verified before use — the brochure-listed awards are already verified and safe.

The client described their one-sentence pitch as: "International Standard, Adventure for & Fitness, full body." And they want visitors to feel "Friendly & Educational." This is the emotional brief for every piece of content: it should feel approachable, educational, and quietly impressive — never intimidating, never boastful.

The Four Safe Content Pillars

Every post belongs to one of four pillars. If three consecutive posts fall in the same pillar, stop and shift. Show — facility, sport, equipment, atmosphere — builds desire. Teach — technique, safety, FAQs, programme explainers — builds credibility. Prove — named award winners, verified event history, real testimonials — builds trust. Connect — trainer profiles, beginner journeys, behind-the-scenes — builds loyalty.

Language That Is Safe vs. Language to Avoid

Safe to use: "World Climbing sport," "competition-ready facility," "hosted IFSC Asian Kids Championship," "Shiv Chhatrapati Rajya Krida Puraskar awardees," "15m Lead and Speed wall," "5m Bouldering section," "7 Auto-Belays," "certified trainers," "80% trial-to-membership conversion" (attributed to their own data), "free for PCMC students," "friendly and educational environment," "Yoga Park, Pimple Saudagar, Near Vibgyor School."

Never use without documented proof: "India's largest," "Olympic-certified," "Olympic-standard," "Best in India," "International-grade," "Fully operational," "Guaranteed results," any reference to the dormitory facility, any mention of free pricing for the general public.

WHAT THIS
STRATEGY REQUIRES

These are the operational commitments without which the strategy cannot perform as described. They come directly from the gaps identified in the survey — not from general best practices, but from what the data specifically revealed about this client and this facility.

Operational Commitments Required

  • Single content owner must be named. The survey showed social media is managed by the operator, the municipal body, and both — with needs for clarification. Shared ownership without a named lead is the most common reason posting consistency collapses by Week 3. One person holds the calendar and the posting access. This week, not next week.
  • DMs and calls answered within 24 hours at minimum, 4–6 hours when campaigns run. The 80% trial conversion happens through instructor interaction — which means every unanswered enquiry is a lost membership. The campaign can bring people to the door; only the team can get them inside.
  • Written filming and photography permissions must be obtained before any content shoots involving identifiable people. The survey confirmed these do not currently exist. This applies especially to minors. No Reels featuring children should be published until parental consent forms are signed and filed. This is both a legal requirement and a reputational protection.
  • Google Business hours must be 100% accurate at all times. A user who arrives when the listing says "Open" but finds the facility closed will write a negative review that can take months to recover from. During exam season (when the survey confirmed attendance drops), hours should be verified and updated proactively.
  • Achievements and awards must be verified before use in any public content. The survey stated this explicitly. The brochure content — named awardees, named trainers, verified event history — is already cleared. Anything beyond the brochure requires confirmation before publication.
  • A monthly performance report must be shared with the client at the end of each 30-day block. The client's trust signal is "digi footprint" — they need to see it growing in measurable form. A simple one-page report showing follower growth, Google review count, Google Business views, and post reach will address the 2-month expectation timeline the client stated in the survey.
  • The dormitory stay must not appear in any public content. This was explicitly stated in the survey handwritten notes. No mention in captions, stories, reels, or website copy until further instruction from the client.

What This Strategy Will and Will Not Do

  • This strategy will build a consistent, credible, content-rich digital presence for PCMC Sport Climbing Complex across Instagram, Facebook, Google, and YouTube over 90 days.
  • This strategy will not produce viral content, guaranteed follower counts, or contractual membership numbers. Social media growth for a local physical sports facility compounds over months, not weeks.
  • This strategy will not replace the in-person experience. The survey confirmed first impressions are excellent ("Lai bhari"). The digital strategy's sole job is to get people through the door — the 80% conversion rate takes care of the rest.
  • This strategy treats the 2-month expectation the client expressed as a deadline for a measurable progress report, not a deadline for viral growth. The report at the end of Month 2 should clearly show the growing digital footprint the client defined as their trust metric.
  • After Month 3, a second-phase strategy should be built using the data collected — potentially including school-outreach digital campaigns, influencer partnerships within the climbing community, and event-specific paid promotions aligned with competition calendar dates.
